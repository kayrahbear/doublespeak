What is it?

Doublespeak is a project focused on natural language processing with Python, specifically sentiment analysis of various presidential candidates. Doublespeak takes campaign speeches from presidential candidates, breaks them into individual sentences, and then gauges the general sentiment(positive/negative) of each candidate's campaign with the . The IBM Watson Tone Analysis API is then utilized to expand the general sentiment into personality and emotional tone analysis.

How does it work?

Variations of the Naive Bayes algorithm were trained with positive and negative movie reviews and tweets in order to increase and stabilize the fluctuating base accuracy of the algorithm. These newly trained algorithms were then pickled to increase the speed of the sentiment analysis.

The NLTK (Natural Language Toolkit) library was utilized to 'tokenize' (split) each candidate's speeches into sentences before analyzing them for positive/negative sentiment with the trained bayesian algorithms. A mean of all the algorithm's accuracy ratings is presented alonside the sentiment decision as a 'confidence' score.

Why 'Doublespeak'?

The term doublespeak is defined as 'intentionally deceiving language. Not an outright lie or a tactful euphemism, but systematic use of ambiguous, evasive words and sentence structures to say one thing but mean something else'. Commonly associated with bureaucracy, military, and politics, it is often used as a calculated attempt to avoid or shift responsibility, distort reality by making the bad, negative, or unpleasant look good, positive, or pleasant, and vice versa, and confuse by using unfamiliar or concocted jargon.
